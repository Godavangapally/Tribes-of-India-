History


Tripura is a state located in the northeastern part of India, bordered by Bangladesh to the west, north, and south, and the Indian states of Assam and Mizoram to the east.its hilly terrain, dense forests, and picturesque landscapes. It is situated in the eastern foothills of the Himalayas, with the majority of the state covered by hills and valleys. The state's largest city and capital is Agartala.Tripura has a rich history dating back to ancient times. It was ruled by various indigenous tribes, including the Tripuri, Chakma, and Reang, before coming under the influence of the Bengali kings in the medieval period. The state later became a princely state under the Manikya dynasty until it acceded to the Indian Union in 1949.the tribes of Tripura are an integral part of the state's cultural heritage, socio-economic fabric, and political landscape. Their contributions to preserving cultural heritage, promoting economic development, fostering social cohesion, and enriching the tourism industry highlight their importance in the overall development of Tripura. Tripura has a diverse population comprising various ethnic groups and communities. The majority of the population belongs to indigenous tribes such as the Tripuri, Reang, Chakma, and Jamatia. Bengali is the predominant language spoken in the state, followed by Kokborok, which is the language of the indigenous Tripuri people.Tripura, a state located in northeastern India, is home to several indigenous tribes, each with its own unique culture, traditions, and language. List of Popular tribes in TRIPURA


  • Tripuri Tribe


    The Tripuri tribe is the largest ethnic group in Tripura, comprising the majority of the state's population. They are also known as Tipra or Tripura people. The Tripuri tribe has a rich cultural heritage, including traditional dance forms such as Garia and Lebang Boomani, as well as festivals like Kharchi Puja and Garia Puja. The Kokborok language, belonging to the Tibeto-Burman language family, is spoken by the Tripuri people.


  • Reang (Bru):


    The Reang tribe, also known as Bru, is one of the major indigenous tribes of Tripura. They primarily inhabit the districts of Dhalai and North Tripura. The Reang people have a distinct culture, including traditional music and dance forms such as Hojagiri and Garia dance. The Reang language is a Tibeto-Burman language spoken by the tribe.


  • Chakma Tribe


    The Chakma tribe is one of the largest ethnic groups in Tripura, mainly concentrated in the districts of South Tripura and Dhalai. They have a rich cultural heritage, including traditional festivals like Buddha Purnima and Bishu, as well as handicrafts such as weaving and bamboo work. The Chakma language, belonging to the Tibeto-Burman language family, is spoken by the Chakma people.
