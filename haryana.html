HARYANA





History


Haryana, a state in northern India, boasts a rich history that spans ancient civilizations, medieval dynasties, and its emergence as a separate entity in 1966. Home to diverse tribal communities, the state has endeavored to preserve and promote their unique cultural heritage. Haryana's contributions include initiatives for economic empowerment, skill development, and entrepreneurship opportunities for tribal groups. The government has focused on improving education and healthcare facilities in tribal areas, ensuring access to resources and addressing land rights and rehabilitation issues. Various welfare schemes and reservation policies underscore the state's commitment to the well-being and inclusion of tribal communities, with ongoing efforts to uplift and support them in their socio-economic development.

    The Chodhari are not aggressive and use weapons only for hunting or defending themselves against robbers or wild animals. They are generally known to be honest, uninhibited, simple, likable people. Most of the Chodhari work as small-time farmers. Cotton, vegetables and rice are their principal crops.


  • Barda Tribe


    The Barda tribe is also known as Adivasi or Khandeshi Bhil. They are strictly endogamous and practice clan exogamy. Their major clans are the Ahir, Baria, Dania, Gaikwad, Mali, Mori, and Thakur, all of whom intermarry
