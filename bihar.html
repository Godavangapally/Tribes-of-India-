BIHAR





History


Bihar is a state in Eastern India. It is the third largest state by population, the 12th largest by area, and the 14th largest by GDP in 2021. Bihar borders Uttar Pradesh to its west, Nepal to the north, the northern part of West Bengal to the east, and Jharkhand to the south. The Bihar plain is split by the river Ganges, which flows from west to east.On 15 November 2000, southern Bihar was ceded to form the new state of Jharkhand.Only 11.27% of the population of Bihar lives in urban areas as per a 2020 report. Additionally, almost 58% of Biharis are below the age of 25, giving Bihar the highest proportion of young people of any Indian state.



List of Popular tribes in BIHAR


  • Kharia Tribe


    The Kharia tribe is an indigenous community primarily residing in the eastern Indian states of Bihar, Jharkhand, and Odisha. They have their own distinct language, Kharia, which belongs to the Munda subgroup of the Austroasiatic language family. Traditionally, the Kharia people have been engaged in agriculture, hunting, and gathering, with a strong dependence on forest resources for their livelihoods. They have a rich oral tradition, including folk tales, songs, and rituals that are integral to their cultural identity. Despite facing challenges such as land dispossession and socio-economic marginalization, the Kharia tribe continues to maintain its cultural heritage and resilience in the face of modernization.

  • Santhal Tribe


    The Santhal tribe is one of the largest indigenous communities in Jharkhand, India, with a rich cultural heritage. They primarily inhabit the Chota Nagpur Plateau region and are also found in parts of West Bengal, Bihar, and Odisha. The Santhals have their own language, Santhali, which belongs to the Austroasiatic language family. Traditionally, they are known for their agricultural practices, particularly rice cultivation, as well as hunting and gathering. Santhal society is characterized by a strong sense of community, with traditional customs, rituals, and festivals playing a significant role in their social fabric.


  • Gond


    The Gond tribe, a significant presence in Telangana, has a rich cultural heritage, including unique Gondi language, traditional art, and vibrant folklore. They often inhabit forested areas and contribute to the state's cultural diversity.
